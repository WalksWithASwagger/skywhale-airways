# Origin Notes — *A Psychedelic Airport for Time Travelers*

Genesis / creative-development record for the historical archive. Captures how
the order, script, and soundtrack were arrived at (brainstorm by Kris Krüg with
an AI collaborator), before the Veo/edit production pass.

## Premise

A lone traveler passes through a surreal airport where **fish function as
aircraft, waiting rooms split time, luggage has memory, and every terminal
belongs to a different decade.**

## How the 10 keyframes were ordered

Images were given letter codes, then ordered so visual *overlaps became
escalation* (two big terminals, two airborne shots, two procedural spaces — each
turned into a distinct chapter instead of a duplicate).

Final order (letter → scene → our s-id):

| # | Letter | Scene | s-id |
|---|--------|-------|------|
| 1 | H | Giant yellow skywhale (establishing) | s01 |
| 2 | F | Lost-baggage check-in | s02 |
| 3 | A | Red-ceiling departures concourse | s03 |
| 4 | J | Pink waiting room / duplicate selves | s04 |
| 5 | D | Boarding / portal platform | s05 |
| 6 | G | Zero-gravity transfer (lift-off) | s06 |
| 7 | I | Riding the golden fish (hero flight) | s07 |
| 8 | E | Holding-pattern runway / tarmac limbo | s08 |
| 9 | C | Cloud-orchard layover | s09 |
| 10 | B | Pink rotunda arrival | s10 |

Arc: **Act I entry** (world → check-in → concourse → time fractures) → **Act II
transit** (boarding → lift-off → flight → holding pattern) → **Act III arrival**
(layover orchard → arrival in another decade).

Five anchor images carry the film: **H, A, J, I, B.**

## Script evolution

1. **v1 — explanatory** ("there are airports for cities…"). Clear but
   tour-guide-ish; explained the movie too much.
2. **v2 — gonzo-lyrical** ("we came in low under a yellow god with fins"). More
   sensation than plot; let the images do the nouns, the voice do the weather.
3. **v3 — shipped: male voice, third person, ≤60s.** Subject is female but the
   song is performed by a male narrator (hence she/her in the lyric).

### Shipped 60-second voiceover (third person, male)

> She came in low under a yellow god with fins.
> The runway was already dreaming.
> Somebody had taught the fish to keep schedules, and the schedules had gone strange.
> She carried one bag, one face, and a pocket full of wrong minutes.
> Inside, the terminal breathed pink light.
> At the gate, she saw herself already waiting.
> Then gravity became a rumour.
> The floor gave up. The chairs gave up. Time loosened its tie.
> She lifted sideways through fish traffic and airport perfume.
> The big yellow animal did not ask where she was going. Good pilots never do.
> She arrived with one bag, one body, and tomorrow's yesterday.

This is the narration baked into the **`whale sky god.mp3`** track used in the
final cut (so no separate VO is layered).

### The "little knives" (favorite lines to protect)

- "somebody had taught the fish to keep schedules, and the schedules had gone bad in a beautiful way."
- "i had one bag, one face, and a pocket full of wrong minutes."
- "at the gate, i saw myself already delayed."
- "gravity was only a rumour here." / "we lifted sideways."
- "good pilots never do."
- "chairs waiting for people who had missed themselves."
- "a boarding pass stamped with tomorrow's yesterday."

## Suno learnings (music: "whale sky god")

- **Style field cap ~1000 chars.** Best pattern: genre + mood + tempo +
  instruments + vocal delivery + production texture, compact and layered.
- **To force short-form** (it kept landing ~145s): explicitly state
  "short-form 45–60s, start on voice, no long intro, no instrumental bridge, no
  big outro," and keep the spoken lyric lean (~95–120 words). Spoken word gets
  stretched more than expected.
- Put extra context in the lyrics box, not the style field.

### Dialed-in Suno style prompt (the one that worked)

> Short-form 45–60 second spoken-word cinematic track. Male voice, calm, low,
> dry, intimate, lightly haunted. Gonzo-poetic retro travelogue, not sung, no
> chorus. 76–82 BPM psychedelic lounge-jazz, warm analog tape texture. Brushed
> drums, upright bass, Rhodes, vibraphone, distant trumpet, soft theremin,
> airport chimes through tape delay, watery bubbles, reel hiss, vinyl crackle.
> Mood: 1970s airport film dissolving into a fish dream. Start immediately with
> voice, no long intro, no instrumental bridge, no big outro. Keep voice clear
> and forward, music airy underneath. Avoid EDM, pop vocals, trailer drums,
> sentimental piano, comedy music.

## Suno style note (longer, pre-1000-char-cap version, for reference)

Spoken-word cinematic narration over retro-futurist psychedelic lounge jazz,
warm analog tape. Calm low male narrator, intimate underground-radio delivery,
long pauses, lightly haunted, dry wit, no melodrama. Bed: brushed drums,
vibraphone, upright bass, Rhodes, soft theremin, distant trumpet, analog pads,
tape-delay airport chimes, watery bubbles, reel-to-reel hiss, vinyl crackle.
1970s airline brochure discovered in a dream. Voice forward, music airy. No EDM,
no pop vocals, no trailer drums.

## Per-image regeneration candidates (noted, mostly unused — pro pass solved it)

- **F luggage hall** — signage was gibberish; could add readable "LOST BAGGAGE".
- **J waiting room** — sharpen the duplicate-self idea; optional "GATE DRIFT".
- **E holding pattern** — clearer runway markings / route maps.
- **B arrival rotunda** — slightly more ceremonial / a doorway to imply destination.

Text philosophy: keep readable text **sparse** — one or two anchors; let most
signage stay dreamlike / semi-legible.
