VOICEOVER DRAFT
There are airports for cities, airports for countries, and one airport for lost decades.

Beneath the skywhale, she arrives carrying a single bag.

At check-in, they weigh her luggage, her memories, and the years she has not yet unpacked.

In the departures hall, fish move overhead and the clock refuses ordinary time.

At the gate, she meets another version of herself already waiting.

When boarding begins, the runway opens like a memory.

Somewhere between gate and sky, gravity stops insisting.

A golden fish carries her between decades.

They do not arrive. They circle. Every decade has its own weather.

In the layover orchard, even the clouds are waiting for someone.

At last she enters a vast terminal where fish drift through the ceiling like constellations.

She has arrived in another decade, carrying one bag, one body, and more time than she left with.
